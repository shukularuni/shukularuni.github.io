AI isn't going anywhere, so what now?

Shukularuni, 19/04/2026.

The future of artificial intelligence is something that has been bugging my mind for these past few years, this is because I don't really know to where some of its aspects are heading. Specifically about what is known as generative artificial intelligence or gen AI, which has had such a quick growth that makes it seem like the companies that manage these models, look like they prioritize scale over necessity. But at the same time, it seems almost impossible to imagine a future without a trace of this new technology, because AI has an incredible advantage for every not cool disadvantage, but just pretending that a bubble will pop or that it will somehow just completely disappear out of thin air is not realistic nor productive.

And of course, there are places where AI has shown its value in ways that are difficult to argue with; one of the most important examples of this is based on scientific research, like the prediction of new proteins, or the fast advances in the field of cancer research. This is exactly the type of AI that should stay and the one we need to keep developing, because it has the potential to save actual lives, these models are capable of looking through colossal amounts of data and finding patterns that would've taken these researchers years, decades even. What I'm trying to say here is that, this is a tool that extends the capacity of the person using it, and it's not replacing them entirely. So, in this way, AI is basically a very significant tool for those that might need it the most.

But again; generative AI is a more complicated issue than this, it's obviously very powerful, and it's honestly so impressive how it's gotten so good so quickly, and in some ways useful I guess. Although it's such cool technology as an idea, yet it's sitting at my #1 spot of my most hated stuff in the entire universe, this is because of its very little problem of resource consumption, because to operate at the massive scale it's doing right now, it needs many many many resources, all the way from computational power to the freaking water you're sipping right now just to maintain itself cold, and no, they won't use a better solution because it's obviously cheaper to just suck up all the water in the world, hah! That rant aside, even though it needs so many resources, the problem is that there is too much investment into the "new revolutionary technology", too much public interest, and too many weird applications for generative AI, for it to just disappear. It's because this technology has already engrained itself so much into the landscape that the internet is, that whether you wanted it or not, and it's not going anywhere soon.

Well, hold on there! Before we continue, why don't we take a quick break to make some context, because this has already happened before, hear me out. If we take out our history books to look at the past how other new innovative technologies have evolved, everything starts to make a little more sense; when calculators were invented, mathematicians weren't replaced, instead only the ones that adapted to use the new technology were left on the field, and they did, they used the new calculator technology to speed up their work, to be more efficient that is. Similarly, the internet started as a bubble too, it didn't replace teachers, human interaction, nor libraries, instead it gave the world access to more information about everything than you could imagine. It made communication easier through rough times like the pandemic, made it possible to still be able to learn and communicate with your peers despite the distance or the medium of communication. What this has to do with AI is that, new technologies aren't here to replace us or finish us off, it's just a tool that when used rightly can help you a lot more than you'd think.

And it's also very probable that the same pattern might continue for AI, only time will tell, but what's clear as day is that yes, this technology is going to change many industries, it'll make some jobs obsolete, although it'll create more opportunities that hadn't existed before. The idea that AI will replace all jobs completely, excessively oversimplifies the capabilities of this new technology and how complex people's jobs actually are. Saying that AI will end with an entire culture of working is an absolute misunderstanding or just a great exaggeration, because work is not only about efficiency, but also requires creativity, adaptability, and critical thinking, and that is what makes it human.

So, instead of creating fear or extreme predictions that spread misinformation, it'd be much better if people tried taking their time researching a bit about this topic, a great starting point would be about what AI can or can't do. Because like any other tool, there will always be somebody that uses it to do bad stuff or just not use it correctly. And yeah, there are genuine reasons to believe why this must be discussed, like it's colossal resource consumption, and other ethical concerns. These problems should be viewed in a reflexive manner, without creating panic, like if somebody were to see the movie Terminator, and wrongly "predict" the same will happen in real life.

With that said, it's important to mention that nobody, and I mean absolutely nobody knows where the future of AI is heading to, whether you are just a normal person or the boss of an AI company. Technology evolves is weird unpredictable ways, and this evolution is principally made up of factors that can't be predicted, such as investments, human decisions, or literally just cultural acceptance. It's very easy to focus only on the bad side of this new innovative tool, or to just assume the worst, the thing is that it is completely possible that I'll wake up tomorrow and it will have a very significant development that nobody would've seen coming today.

In the end, the future of AI and where it might be heading still remains the biggest mystery of all time. The only thing we can have for certain is that, this is just a new tool, AI isn't going anywhere, it's not going to disappear, but it's also not going to the other extreme and ending with the world like in Terminator, it's going to stay somewhere in between these two extreme points. AI is here to stay, so instead of rejecting it or saying that it's over for us, we should stay informed and think critically. What happens in the end is not going to depend on AI itself, but on certain factors that we can't predict to this day, and just a small fraction of these many factors, are controlled by us humans. So instead of asking what will be the future of AI, try to ask; how can we mold this new technology bit by bit so that it becomes something I want to see in my future.

And for the people that might have lost a job because of generative AI; I'm not against you, I think that whoever decided that, doesn't actually understand the complexity that comes with such jobs as yours. And to trust such a new and fallible underdeveloped technology is definitely the result of cutting corners. And that says a lot more about the priorities of whoever made that call than it does about the value of what you were doing, because as I've been saying this whole article, if your role required judgment, critical thinking, and human understanding, then there's literally no AI model that can replace you.
