Theory

It's hexadecimal again, but we know from the previous challenge that it's because they're multiple layers of encryption. So, looking at the structure of the string, we can see a couple of binary characters at the start that remind me of Gzip compression. So let's decode it.

Solution

Let's put it into CyberChef to decode from Hex to check what it really is:

recipe=From_Hex('Space')  # hex-to-text

- INPUT: 1f 8b 08 08 f9 fc 70 5e 00 ff 48 69 6e 74 3a 20 44 65 74 65 63 74 20 46 69 6c 65 20 54 79 70 ...

- OUTPUT:
...uup^.yHint: Detect File Type.U.=rA ..{.b3

Yup, just as I thought, it's Gzip compression. That binary start and the comment of the file to check file type just give it away, so let's decompress from Gunzip:

recipe=From_Hex('Space')Gunzip()  # hex-to-text and gzip decompress

- INPUT: 1f 8b 08 08 f9 fc 70 5e 00 ff 48 69 6e 74 3a 20 44 65 74 65 63 74 20 46 69 6c 65 20 54 79 70 ...

- OUTPUT:
