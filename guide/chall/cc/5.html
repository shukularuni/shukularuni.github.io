Theory

It's hexadecimal again, but we know from the previous challenge that it's because they're multiple layers of encryption. So, looking at the structure of the string, we can see a couple of hex values for letters at the start, that might be interesting to look into. So let's decode it.

Solution

Let's put it into CyberChef to decode from Hex to check what it really is:

recipe=From_Hex('Space')  # hex-to-text

- INPUT: 42 5a 68 39 31 41 59 26 53 59 34 3d 45 44 3d 31 37 3d 44 45 3d 30 30 3d 30 30 28 3d 44 46 3d ...

- OUTPUT:
BZh91AY&SY4=ED=17=DE=00=00(=DF=80=00=10h=...

Oh look, this is Bzip2. To decode it we need to make this the correct format, we can just use the quopri module of python and put it in a bzip file:

~$ nano idk.py
